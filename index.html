HISTORY OF THE SCHOOL

San Buenaventura National High School – Extension is the former name of the school, now San Buenaventura Integrated National High School – Annex. It is located at Estrellado St. Brgy. Zone V, Luisiana, Laguna. It was pushed through the effort of Hon. Mayor Manuel R. Rondilla, Sangguniang Bayan and Chairmen of the Barangays that benefit the parents who really want their children to finish their high school with less expenses. The school principal that time was Mrs. Andrea T. Apaya and they opened it to the public on June 4, 2007, under Sangguniang Bayan Resolution No. 204-10. It was embraced by the public and got one hundred four (104) first year students. The classes were held temporarily at the three (3) buildings. The Bagong Lipunan (LCES elementary buildings), MUSCA Building and old SK Building as classroom, canteen, faculty room and library. Maintenance, roofs, student’s chair, teacher’s table and chairs were given/donated also by the Municipality of Luisiana. With continuous support from the Municipality of Luisiana headed by Hon. Mayor Manuel R. Rondilla, one of the teacher was temporarily hired Mr. Gil A. Veloria and three teachers were from San Buenaventura National High School – Main they were Mrs. Antonia S. Raflores, Mrs. Marlene A. Apuada, and Mrs. Florabelle S. David that taught and handled the classes. The establishment of San Buenaventura Extension gave the youth of 15 Barangays and 8 Zones of Luisiana the opportunity to acquire free Secondary education. It was really welcomed and respected by the community since most students belong to the average and below average income earners. It was really an answered prayer for everybody as they would not worry anymore, asking from sponsors as their children have now the equal and free education.

By 2010, three years after its foundation, it was recognized and was granted to be San Buenaventura National High School - Annex. The required requirements for this recognition was passed and met by the joint effort of the teachers and school head. By 2016, the K to 12 curriculum was implemented and the school, San Buenaventura National High School - Annex became Integrated School and begun to cater Senior High School learners that offers the courses Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM), and Technical, Vocational and Livelihood (TVL) track. As the school continue to progress, the increasing number of students and even teachers became perceptible. The Department of Education never fails in supporting the needs of the school and up today, even the stakeholders give their full support on the several projects for San Buenaventura Integrated National High School - Annex. In 2023, the school named Luisiana Integrated National High School.
